Living History: The Fife and Drum Corps Journey

Ongoing Â· 2023-Present

A comprehensive multimedia project documenting my experience as a member of a Fife and Drum Corps. This project combines photography, video, written reflections, and historical research to explore how young people connect with America's revolutionary past through music and ceremony.

Through this work, I examine themes of tradition, identity, community, and the relevance of historical memory in contemporary society. The project serves as both personal documentation and a broader commentary on cultural preservation.

Components:
  • Photo essay documenting rehearsals and performances
  • Interview series with corps members and historians
  • Personal essays on learning and tradition
  • Historical research on Fife and Drum Corps history

Revolutionary Voices: Stories from the Reenactment Community

In Progress Â· 2024-2026

An investigative journalism and photography project exploring the world of historical reenactment. This project seeks to understand why people dedicate themselves to recreating history and what these communities reveal about our relationship with the past.

Through in-depth interviews, observational photography, and participatory research, I'm building a portrait of a community united by passion for living history. The project challenges stereotypes about reenactors while celebrating their dedication to education and preservation.

Planned Outputs:
  • Long-form article series
  • Photography exhibition
  • Documentary short film
  • Oral history archive

Heritage Landscapes: Photographing Historical Sites

Ongoing Â· 2024-Present

A photographic survey of Revolutionary War sites and historical landmarks, exploring how these places evolve over time while maintaining their significance. This project combines landscape photography with historical research to create a visual archive of memory and place.

Each location is photographed across different seasons and times of day, creating a layered portrait that reflects both the permanence of history and the constant change of the natural world. The work raises questions about preservation, memory, and the meaning we ascribe to historical spaces.

Project Goals:
  • Document 50+ historical sites
  • Create a digital archive accessible to educators
  • Produce a coffee table book
  • Develop educational materials for schools
