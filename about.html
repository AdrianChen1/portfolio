Hello, I'm Adrian

I'm a high school student at Belmont Hill School with a deep passion for storytelling, history, and creative expression. As an Eagle Scout with Troop 10 in Lexington, Massachusetts, I've had the privilege of working intimately with the Lexington Historical Society on a meaningful project to refurbish the Buckman Tavern garden ahead of Lexington's 250th anniversary celebrations.

This project was more than just community service‚Äîit was a profound connection to the place where Revolutionary history came alive. Working alongside historians to preserve this historic site deepened my understanding of how physical spaces hold memory and meaning. It's this experience that drives my photography and journalism: the desire to connect with my culture, bridge past and present, and continue this legacy of preservation.

Through my lens and my writing, I explore how revolutionary ideals continue to shape our modern world. My involvement with the Fife and Drum Corps further grounds this work in lived experience, allowing me to embody the traditions I document. I create not just to showcase technical skill, but to honor the stories that shaped our community and to ensure they resonate with future generations.

Fife and Drum Corps

As a member of the William Diamond Fife and Drum Corps based in Lexington, Massachusetts, I've had the honor of preserving and performing Revolutionary War era music across New England. Our corps travels from state to state‚Äîfrom New Hampshire to Connecticut and beyond‚Äîparticipating in musters, parades, and historical reenactments that bring the spirit of 1776 to life.

Since 2023, I've taken part in monthly parades and performances, learning the discipline and dedication required to authentically represent this important piece of American heritage. Through countless hours of practice and performance, I've developed a profound appreciation for how music and ceremony can connect us to our past and unite communities across generations.

This experience directly informs my creative work. The discipline required in musical performance translates to my photography and writing, while the historical context enriches my understanding of the stories I tell. It's not just about playing period music‚Äîit's about embodying and sharing the spirit of revolutionary America and contributing to something larger than myself.
